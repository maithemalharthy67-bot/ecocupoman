How it's made
From nature to your hands

Five clean steps — no plastic, no compromise.

01

Material Collection

We collect plant-based materials such as recycled paper and palm fibers from sustainable sources.

02

Fiber Cleaning & Prep

Separation and washing of individual paper and plant fibers to ensure uniform quality and safety.

04

Mold & Press

Got Questions?
Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about EcoCup — swipe or use the arrows to browse.

🌿
Materials & Ingredients
What exactly is the bottle made of?
EcoCup bottles are made from 100% natural, plant-based materials — primarily compressed palm fibre sourced from date palm fronds, blended with dried plant leaves and recycled paper pulp. These materials are pressed under heat into a durable bottle shape, then finished with a natural eco-coating for water resistance. No plastic, no synthetic chemicals — just nature.
💧
Water Quality & Taste
Does the bottle leave any taste in the water? Does it taste like paper or cardboard?
No — EcoCup bottles are designed with a food-safe inner eco-coating that creates a neutral barrier between the water and the natural materials. The water tastes completely clean. The coating is derived from plant-based waxes, so there is no papery or earthy flavour. Multiple taste tests in Oman confirmed zero detectable aftertaste.
🌱
Disposal & Composting
How do I properly dispose of the Nature Return bottle? Can I just bury it in my garden?
Yes! The simplest way is to bury it in soil — your garden, a plant pot, or any agricultural land. The palm fibre and plant-leaf materials naturally break down and actually enrich the soil as they decompose. You can also place it in a compost bin or simply leave it in moist earth. The eco-coating is fully biodegradable. No special steps needed — nature does the rest.
⏱️
Environment & Climate
How long does it take to decompose in Omani soil and climate?
In Oman's warm, arid climate the process is remarkably fast. Under typical conditions — buried in moist soil or a garden — an EcoCup Nature Return bottle fully decomposes in approximately 60–90 days. The heat and microbial activity in Omani soil accelerate the breakdown significantly. By contrast, a standard plastic bottle takes over 450 years and never truly disappears — it just becomes microplastics.
🚚
Delivery & Shipping
Do you deliver to all governorates in Oman?
Yes — we deliver across all governorates in Oman, including Muscat, Dhofar, Al Batinah, Ad Dakhiliyah, Ash Sharqiyah, Al Buraymi, Musandam, and Al Wusta. We work with trusted local postal offices and logistics partners to ensure your EcoCup order reaches you wherever you are in the Sultanate. Delivery typically takes 3–5 business days.
🏢
Business & Customisation
Do you offer branded cups with my company logo?
Absolutely! You can fully customise your EcoCup with your company logo, brand colours, and custom messaging — directly printed or engraved on the bottle using eco-friendly ink. It is a great way to show your organisation's commitment to sustainability while promoting your brand. Contact us through the form below or email us to discuss minimum quantities, design options, and pricing.
🏔️
Long Life Bottle · Outdoor Adventures
Is the Long Life bottle strong enough for hiking and outdoor adventures in the desert or wadis?
Yes — the Long Life Bottle is our most rugged and durable model. It is built with a multi-layer palm fibre core reinforced with compressed plant materials, making it resistant to bumps, drops, and rough terrain. The premium eco-coating protects against moisture and desert heat. Whether you are hiking through Wadi Shab, Wadi Bani Khalid, or exploring the dunes of Wahiba Sands, the Long Life Bottle is your ideal sustainable outdoor companion.
